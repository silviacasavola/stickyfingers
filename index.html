Sticky fingers is a project promoted by the Shulman Center, to share the hidden truths of the experience of kleptomaniacs that joined our center.

At the Shulman Center, we serve individuals, couples, families, organizations, and communities affected by shoplifting, Overspending, employee theft and/or hoarding issues. Since 1992, weâ€™ve helped people from around the world through our sensitive and effective assessment, education, and treatment.

Kleptomania is classified as an impulse control disorder characterized by the inability to resist the urge to steal items, usually for reasons other than personal use or financial gain.

During our 30 years of service, we could see how much hurt comes from a general misconception of the topic, and adds up to the difficulties of the disorder itself. Despite its clinical connotation, kleptomaniacs undergo moral scrutiny and are often stereotyped and diminished.

This is why we asked members of our community to share their testimony, highlighting the several sides and collateral implications of their disorders, and how they feel about it. Our goal is to sensitize a broader public, and to make some order in the hank of pre and misconceptions.
If you or someone close to you might need help for kleptomania, contact us at terrenceshulman@theshulmancenter.com.
